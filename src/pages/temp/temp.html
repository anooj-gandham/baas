The quadratures of a quantized field are the position and momentum operators associated with each oscillator. The quadratures satisfy the canonical commutation relation: $$1 = i \hbar$$ where $\hbar$ is the reduced Planck constant. This relation reflects the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, which implies that the more precisely one measures the position of the field, the less precisely one can know its momentum, and vice versa.
